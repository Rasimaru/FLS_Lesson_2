Importance Of English Language In Information Technology

While English is not the most spoken language globally, it is an official language in several countries. The estimated number of people who use English worldwide for regular communication is an estimated 2 Billion. English is also a dominant business language, and it is almost a necessity for one to penetrate the global markets.

In some strange way, people unconsciously use English as a measure of intelligence in business circles. A strong vocabulary exudes intelligence and authority, but a technological or mathematical genius with poor diction might render himself unintelligent.

So why is English essential in Information Technology?

1. Career Advancement

Different studies across the globe show that people with English knowledge are likely to advance in their careers further. Studies have also shown that a more robust command of the English Language leads to higher pay. If you are like most people, you might have come across someone with a poor English language command. You may either have thought the person was rude or not very intelligent. However, this is sadly the state of affairs in information technology circles.

Without English, you might find it difficult negotiating for better opportunities, stating expectations, describing previous job experiences, accomplishments, and other relevant skills.

2. Business Language

Information Technology is the backbone of the commercial world. On the other hand, English is the language of trade, and it, therefore, acts as a bridge between the Commercial world and information technology. Many programming languages are made in the US in English, or even if made in other countries, they are made in English to suit the global market. A good understanding of English is therefore essential if one is to understand these programs. It also allows for smooth communication while using the program and avoiding confusion.

3. Globalization

Most IT companies recruit internationally, leading to people of many nationalities working in the same company. Other companies have discovered that they can lower tax obligations by setting up their companies overseas. This leads to a lot of outsourcing. Employees can also be brought on work visas to a country, and if they have not mastered the language, it may act as a barrier between them and a high-paying job. Language is, therefore, an excellent communication tool for working successfully in a company.

4. Access to Products

The majority of the internet is in English, and therefore the access to products and services can be limited in IT if one does not correctly understand the language. Also, the majority of the websites were created by English speakers. The back end of the website and codes are either English words or their acronyms, making it easier for English speakers to understand.

5. Computer Processing Language

English is the primary IT language, and it has streamlined computer processing. Most programs are in English and might not respond to other programming languages if they were not programmed to understand the languages. Most programs are built with an English base and later upgraded to support other languages. If one wants to modify a program's speech at the base level, they need to understand English.

6. Learning IT

The importance of the English language is not only seen in the IT workspace but also in school. To know English is paramount for every good IT specialist. Towards the end of the 1800s, the teaching philosophy took a radical shift seen even in modern-day schools. There were different methods such as Grammar Translation, Direct Method, and Audio methods. English facilitates the actual education structure allowing learning to take place in these institutions.

Multimedia presentations, cloud computing, video conferencing, and video editing are some ways Information Technology has revolutionized education. It is easier to grasp some of these concepts fundamentally when learning them in English.

7. Staying up to date with the latest trends

In tech, staying up to date with the latest trends is required of you. However, without English knowledge, you have to wait for the content to be translated to your mother language before accessing it. This is one of the hurdles faced by most non-English speaking IT professionals. You will find yourself behind in information access, and by the time you access it, the information might not be relevant.

8. Managing global teams

If you are an IT manager managing global teams, you need a good grasp of the English language. Managing such a team is not a triviality. With a good command of the language, it will be easy to recognize and understand accents from different countries. Different words also have different connotations in various cultural norms. The best advice for you would be to over-communicate with the team, and this will keep both you and your team members in the same information loop.

If you are not a native English speaker, this will improve your English and improve your team's communication and execution. Your development opportunities will be limitless.

Learning English

On the other hand, information technology has made it easy to learn English compared to earlier ages. It is easier now for students to learn English using technology. Many people learned English using IT products, surfing internet sites, recording and playback systems, and other educational broadcasting systems. Information Technology has transformed the world into a global village, making learning more accessible.

As much as learning English can be challenging and time-consuming, it is valuable with very many accompanying opportunities. You can also hire an English essay writer with advanced skills to help make your learning more accessible and fun.

In many countries where English is not widely spoken, you are considered very highly educated. English knowledge opens a lot of work opportunities both at home and abroad. The travel opportunities are limitless, and being a global citizen can fulfill these dreams for you. People in English-speaking countries complain about outsourcing, but it can be a lucrative opportunity for people in other countries. As you can see, English opens a lot of doors that would have otherwise remained closed.
